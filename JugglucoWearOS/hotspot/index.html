Juggluco for Wear OS

Juggluco is now ported to Wear OS. It consists of an adaptation of the smartphone version to the smaller screen of the watch and a watch face that shows the time, the current glucose value received every minute and four slots for complications.

Every sensor has to be initialized by the smartphone version of Juggluco, that sends the sensor data to the watch version. Thereafter, there are two possibilities:

  • Keep the sensor connected with the smartphone and sends the data from the smartphone to the watch by TCP;

  • If you have a watch with good functioning Bluetooth, you can also try a direct connection between watch and sensor.

Exact instructions on how to get the watch working is given in the Smartphone version of Juggluco (>2.3.1) under Left menu-> watch-> Wearos Config -> Help.

Watch-sensor connection

I have only experience with Samsung Galaxy Watch4. With about half of the sensors Watch4 has a good connection, with the other half it had so much connection errors, that I had to switch to an indirect connection with the sensor.

Watch-phone connection

Connecting the sensor indirectly with the watch always worked very well. For this to work there has to be an IP/TCP connection between phone and watch. This can in two ways:

  • Give the phone an external connection. If the phone has an external connection, you can turn off Wi-Fi on the watch. This external connection is not used by Juggluco, but there is a TCP over Bluetooth connection between watch and phone when such an external connection exists. The external connection can be Wi-Fi, mobile data, personal hotspot, bluetooth tethering etc. After switching connection or getting out of reach, the new connection is sometimes not immediately recognized. You can make it recognized by switching Wi-FI, Mobile Data or Bluetooth OFF-and-ON on the phone;

  • Create a portable hotspot on the smartphone and select this portable hotspot as the Wi-Fi connection on your watch. Sometimes this does work for hours without disruption, but there comes a moment that it stops functioning. The watch doesn't establish a connection any more with the portable hotspot. Possibly rebooting the watch helps.

What to do when Juggluco on the watch stops receiving data from Juggluco on the phone?

See that Direct sensor-watch connection is turned off in the phone Left menu->Watch->Wearos Config.

First of all turn Bluetooth On in both watch and phone. Then switch WIFI off and on in one of them, if that doesn't work, try the other or try the same a second time (Turning Bluetooth off and on can also be effective if tcp is redirected through Bluetooth.) Hereafter you sometimes have to go to Left (middle) Menu-> Mirror and press sync in phone and watch.

It is possible that the connection between Juggluco's on watch and phone doesn't work, because it was changed, therefore some screenshots are shown here of a functioning connection. The shown IP is automatically determined and exchanged via Bluetooth and don't need to be specified manually. The label is a watch identifier also shown in Watch->Wearos Config.

If you want to reinstall the watch version, you should remove the existing connection on the phone in Left-middle menu-> Mirror, by touching the connection and pressing Delete. After reinstalling the watch version of Juggluco you can go again to Watch-Wearos Config and press Init Watch app.

It is very important that always enough storage space is available. If at a critical moment the device is out of storage space, it is possible that the some files are not created the right way and you have to reinstall Juggluco again with enough storage space.

Starting a new Sensor

(US-like sensors always need to be started with the Libre 2 app, thereafter do the same as described hereunder.)

When "Direct sensor-watch connection" is turned OFF, everything should be the same as when no watch is connected to the smartphone.

With "Direct sensor-watch connection" ON you should scan the sensor a few times. After warm-up period, the watch should get a Bluetooth connection with the sensor. Sometimes some more scans are needed after the warm-up period. Sometimes this isn't enough, then restarting Juggluco on the watch, by force closing it and starting it again, may help. If this doesn't work, you can turn OFF "Direct sensor-watch connection" and try it that way. Thereafter you can turn "Direct sensor-watch connection" ON again.

How to get it?

You should be able to find Juggluco in Google Play on your watch, not on your phone or computer
